The Interception, Season 1 (Recap)

North Siofera, during the Reign of Aman VII

In the small town of Nalda, surrounded by barren deserts, a ragtag group of travellers were hired to intercept a package. On the journey to the Midnight Forest, many hijinks ensued, including starting a revolution, uncovering a conspiracy against the Althilian king, and discovering a device that lets them explore memories of the past. Enemies were made, alliances were forged (and lost). When at last they reached the Brazen Bridge, the group quickly discovered that the supposed package was, in fact, a high-profile prisoner, and they were promptly framed for his escape. Now in hiding within the Golden City of Althil, the group must work with an underground network to expose the conspiracy and clear their names – or else find some other means to escape their impending death…

Starring:

  • Uda Rosa Ell, a Highborn whose fair looks can charm almost any beholder. A penchant for alcohol, and an occasional tendency to burn all their clothes in a fit of anguish.
  • Ramon Q. Silver, a natural inquisitor who travels the lands writing poetry and learning people’s life stories in invasive detail, with his pet fox Tamsey.
  • Buzze Horndim, a terrifyingly huge one-armed Rork and aspiring beekeeper. Budding interest in the mystic arts.
  • Tawa Hamlutts, a chaotic individual who travels with a pot on his head. Member of the Church of the Fifth Redemption, a very successful multi-level marketing business. Accidentally roped into leading a group of ineffably lucky mercenaries known as the Tower Rangers. Currently learning how to be a sailor.
  • Lewis Cannen, a disgraced ex-police detective of the ACPD. Wields two crossbows he calls ‘guns’. Obsessed with a set of seemingly unconnected cases known as the ‘Cannen Files’, where his refusal to halt investigation led to his dismissal.
  • Staek Bakeob, a largely unseencharacter who made brief cameos in the previous season to seduce various mature / underage women. Successful has taught his hawk how to catch rats, although it is unclear why a hawk wouldn’t know how to do this already.
